Social Media Analytics & BI Dashboard
MADSC201 · Pandas & AI  |  IHEC Carthage  |  2024
Overview

Analysis of social media behavior across 1,000 users and 7 platforms — TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, Snapchat and Pinterest. Delivered through two distinct Power BI dashboards targeting different decision-makers.

📊 Dashboard 1 — Executive View
Users by Apps
Horizontal bar chart. Snapchat leads with 156 users/day, Facebook is lowest at 136. Shows platform reach distribution across the 1,000 users.
Posting Frequency vs. Engagement
Stacked bar. Compares likes/day against posts/day per platform and color. Identifies the optimal posting frequency for maximum engagement.
Platform Usage Overview
Line chart. Daily minutes spent declining from Snapchat (156 min) to Facebook (136 min). Reveals which platforms hold user attention longest.
Engagement Per Platform
Grouped bars showing Posts, Follows and Likes side by side per platform. Instagram and Snapchat show the highest combined engagement.
🎯 Dashboard 2 — Marketing Team View
Content Performance Analysis
Full-width stacked bar plotting likes/day vs posts/day per platform per color. Identifies which platforms reward higher posting frequency — Instagram and TikTok lead in content-to-engagement returns.
Daily Time Spent on Social Media
Horizontal bars. Snapchat 156 min/day vs Facebook 136 min/day. Guides ad budget timing — longer sessions = higher exposure window.
Engagement Ratio by Platform
Stacked bars combining daily minutes, posts and likes. Snapchat shows the highest engagement-to-time ratio, making it ideal for targeted campaigns.
Key Insights
TikTok leads in daily time spent (300+ min/day) but users post less — consumption-first platform
Instagram drives the highest active engagement — most posts, likes and interactions per user
LinkedIn users post the least but follow the most — clear B2B networking platform
Snapchat has a small but highly engaged niche audience — ideal for targeted campaigns
Peak engagement: evenings 6–11pm and weekends — optimal posting windows identified
Strategic recommendation
Recommended cadence: TikTok 3–4×/week (short video), Instagram 4–5×/week (reels + images), LinkedIn 2–3×/week (articles). Budget split: 70% TikTok+Instagram, 20% LinkedIn, 10% niche platforms.

Recommendation System for Online Streaming Platforms
MADSC201 · Machine Learning & AI  |  EU Business School  |  2024
Overview

Goal: reduce churn and increase watch time by improving personalization and content discovery. Built a hybrid recommender that combines collaborative filtering (SVD), content‑based filtering (TF‑IDF + cosine similarity) and K‑Means clustering. Evaluated models using RMSE, MAE and precision‑recall to balance accuracy and diversity.

Business Goal
Retain users, boost watch time, and surface long‑tail content without losing relevance.
Dataset
Netflix Prize data: combined_data_1.txt (ratings) + movie_titles.csv (metadata).
Evaluation
RMSE & MAE for prediction error, precision‑recall for ranking quality.
📊 Data & Exploratory Analysis
Popularity Bias
Ratings concentrate on a small set of titles, creating filter bubbles and limiting discovery of the long tail.
Cold‑Start & Sparsity
Most users have very few ratings, so pure collaborative filtering struggles for new users/items.
🤖 Model Development & Evaluation
Collaborative Filtering (SVD)
Highest accuracy for users with rich history, but weak for cold‑start cases.
Content‑Based Filtering
Strong for new items using metadata, but may narrow diversity without safeguards.
K‑Means Clustering
Groups similar movies to diversify suggestions and prevent repetitive recommendations.
Hybrid System
Best balance of accuracy + diversity, with improved precision‑recall vs. single models.
Business Impact & ROI
Churn Reduction
Projected ~5% decrease in cancellations (≈$300M annual retention impact).
Watch‑Time Growth
Estimated +10% watch time (≈$500M additional revenue potential).
Cost Efficiency
~3% reduction in acquisition cost (≈$150M/year) through better catalog utilization.
Deployment & Ethics
Phased rollout: data prep (3 months), model development (6 months), beta to 5% of users before full launch.
Privacy‑first design with encryption, transparent explanations, and user control over personalization.
Bias mitigation to prevent over‑promotion of mainstream titles and increase discovery.
Stack
Python Pandas Recommender Systems SVD TF‑IDF Cosine Similarity K‑Means EDA
